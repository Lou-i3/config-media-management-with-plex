    Servarr Containers¶

    Radarr, Sonarr, Bazarr, Prowlarr and FlareSolverr are tools collectively refered to as “Arr” or “Arrs”. They are maintained under the Github organization Servarr.

    As they are all maintained by the same team, the process for installation is very similar.

    General Idea¶

    Docker deployment¶

    Deploy with docker-compose¶

    version: "3.9"  

services:

  bazarr: # Bazarr
    image: [image] # (1)!
    container_name: [Your-Container-Name] # (2)!
    ports:
      - "[out-port]:[app-port]"  # (3)!
    volumes: # (4)!
        # Examples: 
      - "/volume1/Medias Lib/Movies:/movies"
      - "/volume1/Medias Lib/TV Shows:/tv"
      - "/volume1/docker/bazarr:/config"
    environment: 
      PUID: 1026 # (5)!
      GUID: 100
      HOME: /root
      LANG: en_US.UTF-8
      LANGUAGE: en_US.UTF-8
      PATH: /usr/local/sbin:/usr/local/bin:/usr/sbin:/usr/bin:/sbin:/bin
      PS1: $$(whoami)@$$(hostname):$$(pwd)\$$
      S6_CMD_WAIT_FOR_SERVICES_MAXTIME: 0
      TERM: xterm
      TZ: Europe/Paris
      XDG_CONFIG_HOME: /config/xdg
    network_mode: bridge
    1. Enter here the name of the image you need
      Find bellow the different available images!
    2. Enter here the name you want for your container
      No spaces!
    3. Enter the port mappings
      On the left the port your container will have on your server
      On the right the port the app inside the container is using
    4. Here are examples of volumes mappings
      Find bellow which volume are needed for your image!
    5. You need to map user credentials with your account
      In ssh console type in id and get the values for PUID and GUID
